Support from the University

The University of Bristol has roughly 2200 postgraduate research students: this figure will include both masters and PhD students. In 2022, the University of Bristol's financial statements showed a profit of £55 million. Can the University afford to financially support its students?
The work of PhD students plays a vital role in the University's research output, which is instrumental in acquiring grants and funding, which makes up 22% of the University's income. Post graduate students also pay tuition fees, with an average of £5,600 per student. For funded students, these tuition fees are covered by the funder: this money has already been allocated for the student's education. If the University were to reduce tuition fees, and return this money to students, what affect would it have on the finances of the student and University?
